Interactive Learning Tools

Engage students with interactive learning tools that promote active participation and hands-on learning. These tools include simulations, games, quizzes, and interactive exercises designed to deepen understanding and make learning more enjoyable. Perfect for fostering problem-solving skills and critical thinking in a dynamic and immersive educational environment.

Affinity Learning

Affinity is designed to create interactive learning scenarios that engage learners and improve learning outcomes. It allows educators to build web-based simulations where learners can practice knowledge acquisition, application, and decision-making. The tool supports various interaction types, including images, videos, audio recordings, and 360-degree photos, making educational content more interactive.

Educators can use Affinity to develop learning modules that incorporate decision branching, multiple-choice questions, and text evaluations. These modules can be integrated into any LMS platform that supports the LTI standard. This integration allows for seamless access and synchronization of completion status, scores, and learner results.

Affinity offers a flexible and engaging way to deliver course content. It enables learners to explore different learning paths based on their decisions, receive real-time feedback, and self-evaluate their understanding of key concepts. This interactive approach helps identify knowledge gaps and enhances the overall learning experience.

DataBasic.io

DataBasic.io is designed to introduce beginners to data concepts through a suite of easy-to-use web tools. These tools, such as WordCounter, WTFcsv, SameDiff, and ConnectTheDots, make working with data fun and accessible, allowing users to analyze text, understand CSV files, compare text files, and explore data connections.

Educators can integrate DataBasic.io into their courses by using its tools to create engaging, hands-on learning activities. For instance, they can have students analyze text data from literature or social media, compare different datasets, or visualize data connections. The platform also offers facilitation videos to help educators run creative data introduction sessions, making it ideal for non-technical audiences.

Go to the tool Price: Free Difficulty: Easy
edpuzzle

Edpuzzle is an edtech tool designed to enhance student engagement and learning through interactive video lessons. It allows educators to create or customize video content by embedding questions, providing instant feedback, and preventing students from skipping parts of the video. This tool supports self-paced learning, making it adaptable to various class structures and grade levels.

Educators can use Edpuzzle to tailor lessons to their students' needs by recording and editing their own videos or transforming any video from the internet into a customized lesson. The platform also offers ready-to-go, standards-aligned lessons created by expert teachers, which can be shared across schools and globally. In higher education, Edpuzzle promotes deeper understanding and retention through bite-sized micro-lessons. It provides instant analytics to help educators differentiate their teaching and make grading effortless.

Formsite

Formsite allows users to build web forms, online surveys, questionnaires, and polls. It also supports easy ecommerce and secure data collection through its powerful features. With options like PayPal Business and PayPal Personal, it facilitates secure global transactions and payment collections.

Educators can use Formsite to create interactive and engaging surveys, quizzes, and feedback forms for their courses. This tool can help in gathering student feedback, conducting assessments, and collecting data for research purposes. By integrating Formsite into their teaching methods, educators can streamline administrative tasks and enhance student engagement.

Educators can use Genially in their courses to create dynamic and interactive learning materials that captivate students' attention. PINGO

(short for: peer instruction for very large groups)

PINGO is a tool designed to facilitate interaction between educators and their audience through live polls It allows users to prepare questions in advance or create polls on-the-fly during their sessions. Participants can join using any internet-connected device without needing to sign up. Responses are optimized for display on large screens, making it suitable for use in presentations or lectures.

Educators can use PINGO in both physical classrooms and online settings. In a physical classroom, they can project the poll results on a screen for everyone to see, fostering real-time engagement and feedback. Online, educators can integrate PINGO into their virtual classrooms, allowing remote students to participate actively in discussions and polls.

The benefits of using PINGO in higher education include enhanced student engagement, immediate feedback on learning goals, and the ability to gather anonymous feedback. This tool supports active learning and helps educators gauge student understanding, making teaching more effective and interactive.
