Avatars

Voki

Voki is an engaging tool that allows users to create customized avatars that can be personalized in appearance and speech, making learning more interactive and fun. Educators can use Voki to create dynamic presentations, manage classroom activities, and monitor student progress. By incorporating Voki into their courses, teachers can spur conversation, debate, and engagement among students, mirroring the online social experience with a focus on learning.

Voki enhances student engagement by making lessons more interactive and enjoyable. It also facilitates better communication and collaboration among students and between students and teachers. Additionally, Voki can help in developing students' digital literacy and presentation skills, which are crucial in today's tech-driven world.

Flashcards

Anki

Anki is a powerful and intelligent flashcard program designed to make remembering things easier and more efficient than traditional study methods. It supports various media types, including images, audio, videos, and scientific markup, making it versatile for different learning needs. Anki's key feature is its use of spaced repetition, which ensures that users only review material they are about to forget, optimizing study time and retention.

Educators can integrate Anki into their courses by creating custom flashcard decks tailored to their curriculum. These decks can include multimedia elements to enhance learning and engagement. Teachers can also share these decks with students, allowing for consistent and synchronized study materials across different devices. Anki's flexibility in card layout and review timing enables educators to customize the learning experience to suit their students' needs.

Anki helps students manage large volumes of information efficiently, which is crucial for subjects requiring extensive memorization, such as medicine and law. By using Anki, students can improve their retention rates, reduce study time, and ultimately enhance their academic performance. The open-source nature of Anki ensures that it remains accessible and adaptable to various educational contexts.

Tutorials/Guides

Scribe

Scribe is designed to create visual guides quickly and efficiently. It allows educators to generate step-by-step instructions with annotated screenshots and text, making it easier to explain complex processes. Educators can use Scribe in their courses to create detailed guides for software usage, lab procedures, or any other step-by-step tasks. These guides can be shared with students, providing clear, visual instructions they can follow at their own pace. Scribe enhances the clarity of instructional materials, reducing the need for repetitive explanations, and supporting self-paced learning, which can lead to improved student outcomes.

**By installing the desktop app, steps can also be captured outside of the web browser.

Tango

Tango is designed to streamline software training by creating interactive guides directly within the software interface. It allows educators to generate step-by-step tutorials with screenshots, annotations, and descriptions simply by performing the process themselves. These guides can be pinned to the exact location in the software where users need them, making it easier for students to follow along and complete tasks accurately.

Educators can use Tango in their courses to provide real-time, contextual help to students as they navigate complex software. By embedding these guides into learning management systems, knowledge bases, or even chat systems, instructors can ensure that students have access to the necessary resources exactly when and where they need them. This reduces the need for repetitive explanations and allows students to learn at their own pace.

Tango also enhances the onboarding process for new students, making them productive more quickly. It also standardizes processes, reducing errors and increasing efficiency. Additionally, Tango promotes self-service learning, which can significantly reduce the number of support tickets and back-and-forth communications, allowing educators to focus on more critical teaching tasks.
